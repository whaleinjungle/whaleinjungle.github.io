a metaphor of senses

There’s an allegory in Zhuangzi’s book. In the chapter “The Way of Heaven”, Shu(倏), the god of the South Sea, Hu(忽), the god of the North Sea, they often visited Hundun(混沌)–the god of the Central Land. Hundun was a kind and generous host. Shu and Hu wanted to repay his hospitality.

They started to think what Hundun will need. Noticing that Hundun lacked the seven openings(eyes, ears, nostrils, mouth)–the usual senses of sight, hearing, smell and speech, as an act of kindness, they decided to help Hundun by boring one hole a day into its body.

On the seventh day, when the final hole was made. Hundun died.

what is sentient?

We are created with senses. Driven by it, managed by it, lost in it. Being sentient is to be capable of feeling. In modern context, sensory awareness, emotional or conscious life formed another layer of it. We intend to prove our subjectivity by language, feelings, and experiences.

live in tandem, not entangled

Reality and truth have been a myth since time immemorial. Oftentimes, chastened when obsessed with idea of knowing oneself, the dissolution of true self. We live alongside our senses, through which forced structure and norms may block the path to liberation. Motion in unity, dwells sentient live forms.
