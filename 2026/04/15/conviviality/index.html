conviviality

Posted at 2026-04-15 philosophy 

The capacity of people to live and interact together across difference — sharing space, pleasure, and social bonds despite divisions of race, class, religion, or ethnicity.

The Term’s Theoretical Roots
The concept draws most prominently from the cultural theorist Paul Gilroy, who used it to describe how multicultural urban life produces informal, everyday moments of cross-cultural coexistence — not as a utopian erasure of difference, but as a messy, lived negotiation of it. It is distinct from multiculturalism as policy; conviviality is something that happens in practice, on the street, in shared spaces.
